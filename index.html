★ Headline · PFL Austin

A signature on-the-night activation: the attendees receive a MyPrize-branded LED wristband on entry, turning the arena into a synchronised sea of MyPrize blue for TV cameras and social capture.

  • Universal distribution — co-branded LED wristbands for the attendees at PFL Austin (or alternate event TBC between parties)

  • Winners receive co-branded PFL & MyPrize merch, presented on the night
  • TV production awareness — broadcast cut-ins to the lit-up fan moments, building three storylines through the card
  • Consistent visual branding across the arena — synced colour, logo lockup, fan-led activation

01
Partner Access — Meta
Appendix

How the shared Meta access works in practice — scope, post appearance, and data visibility.

Meta Account

Partner access grants visibility into the relevant data in Meta for the specifically partnered “campaign” — the content MyPrize and PFL run together for this pilot. The access allows MyPrize to create the campaign and track its performance. MyPrize does not see anything inside the PFL accounts and is partnered only on the shared campaign where the partnership creative runs.

How Posts Appear

Posts appear as standard sponsored or collaborative content within the specific placements on Instagram and Facebook. From a user perspective, the content appears in feed the same as any other professionally produced brand-partnership ad. Content does not live on the PFL account grid or PFL page — only as pushed content on targeted users' feeds. MyPrize and PFL brands are integrated using assets built from fight-night content: fighters, prize moments, and additional creative gathered on site and from PFL highlights.

PFL Data Visibility

Shared access is limited to campaign-level performance data for the MyPrize-PFL partnership. Metrics include impressions, reach, engagement, click-through rates, and MyPrize conversion activity associated with the campaign. No PFL-specific data, underlying databases, or sensitive data is accessible in any way.
